## The Economic Rationale of Cities
The primary purpose of a city is to facilitate the exchange of goods, services, and information [10]. A city is typically defined as an urban area through which people can commute on a daily basis. Larger cities tend to create more wealth by offering wider job markets, labor pools, and diversity of products for sale.
