# Waste Composition

The composition of municipal solid waste is estimated as follows.

<ArticleImage image={"waste_composition.svg"} width={750} height={400} />
#### High level categories are determined from [^3]. The division of plastic is estimated from [^1] and for metals and paper from [^2]. 'Other nonferrous metals' reported by [^2] are assumed to be copper.

[^1]: Aurpa, S. S. ["Characterization of MSW and Plastic Waste Volume Estimation During COVID-19 Pandemic"](https://rc.library.uta.edu/uta-ir/bitstream/handle/10106/29764/AURPA-THESIS-2021.pdf?sequence=1&isAllowed=y). Masters Thesis, University of Texas at Arlington. May 2021.

[^2]: Spendlow, P. ["Oregon solid waste characterization and composition study"](https://www.oregon.gov/deq/mm/pages/waste-composition-study.aspx). July 2018.

[^3]: United States Environmental Protection Agency. ["National Overview: Facts and Figures on Materials, Wastes and Recycling"](https://www.epa.gov/facts-and-figures-about-materials-waste-and-recycling/national-overview-facts-and-figures-materials). Accessed November 8, 2021.
