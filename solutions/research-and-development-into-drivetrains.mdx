## Description

We recommend full funding into research and development for wind turbines.

## Background

Wind power has advanced considerably in recent years, both in terms of market share and technology, but further development is necessary for wind to achieve its full potential. Five key areas for research and development have been identified: understanding of atmospheric physics, turbine design, grid integration, environmental design, and social aspects [^"3"]. Advancement in atmospheric is important so as to better forecast and utilize wind resources [^"54"], especially for offshore wind power [^"3"].

Drivetrains are one particular area in which research is needed. The drivetrain is the power conversion system that converts the kinetic energy of the wind into electrical energy [^"1"]. The main challenges in drivetrain research include drivetrain dynamic responses in large or floating turbines, aerodynamic and farm control effects, use of rare-earth material in generators, improving reliability through prognostics, and use of advances in digitalization [^"1"].
Monitoring has been, and will continue to be, an important part of these efforts [^"46"].

## Implementation

The Wind Energy Technologies Office within the U.S. Department of Energy has been a major driver of R&D funding. Major initiatives include the Wind Forecast Improvement Project [^"5"], grid integration [^"7"], next-generation turbines [^"8"], offshore wind turbines [^"9"], wind turbine recycling [^"10"], and reduction of collisions with birds and bats [^"11"]. The International Energy Agency also coordinates research in atmospheric physics and in other areas [^"6"].

In 2025, the Fiscal Year 2026 budget request was released, with proposes no funding for the Wind Energy Technologies Office [^"12"]. Funding levels had ranged from $104 million to $137 million from 2020 to 2024. We propose that funding levels remain above $100 million per year.

In 2007, the National Renewable Energy Laboratory established the Drivetrain Reliability Collaborative to bring together government and industry to improve drivetrain lifespan and reduce wind turbine maintenance needs [^"25"]. The Gearbox Reliability Collaborative, focusing on gearboxes, was also established [^"25"]. Such efforts should also continue to be fully funded.
